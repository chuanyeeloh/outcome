  5. Describe common development methodologies used by modern project managers and identify its advantages and disadvantages.

    6. Software development methodologies is the important part of developing the software. There are a lot of advantages and disadvantages on the software development methodologies.
    The waterfall is one of the most traditional and commonly used by software development methodologies for software development. The advantages of waterfall model are it is very simple and easy to understand. Next, in this water fall phases are processed and completed are at once in a time so it can save a lot of time. The disadvantages of waterfall are once an application is in the testing stage, it is not possible to go back and edit again. More, waterfall is good for a small project but not suitable for a long and huge projects.
    The prototype methodology is the software development process which allows developers to create only the prototype of the solution to demonstrate its functionality to the clients and make necessary modifications before developing the actual application. The advantages of prototype methodology are when a prototype is shown to the clients, they get a clear understanding and complete 'feel' of the functionality of the software. The disadvantages of prototype methodology are too much involvement of client is not always favoured by the software developer and too many modifications may not good for the project, as it easily disturbs the workflow of the entire software development team.
